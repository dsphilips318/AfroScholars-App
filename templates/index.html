Welcome to AfroScholars

AfroScholars is a West African Language Learning app. At AfroScholars we encourage the embracement of West African culture through learning languages that are indigenous to West African countries.

Meet the Founders

Daniel

A rising sophomore who loves sports, coding, and playing instruments.

Daniel is of Nigerian descent and started learning about his native language, Yoruba, at a young age.

Maimouna

A rising senior who has a passion for learning indigenous languages. Her struggle with learning her native tongue, Bambara, a language spoken in Mali, allowed her to realize the importance of learning languages to connect to her culture.

Ziara

A rising sophomore who loves lacrosse, fashion, and business.

Ever since Ziara immigrated from the Dominican Republic to the US, she lost her native language, Spanish, and is struggling to regain her former connection with her native culture.

Jayden

A rising sophomore who enjoys reading and drawing.

Jayden doesn't speak any West African languages but he can relate to this problem as well. His grandparents speak an uncommon Chinese dialect so he has a hard time learning it.

Why Do We Do This?

A lot of African languages are spoken only. Most of those languages' text have been destroyed. Because of this there is a lack of representation of African languages on language learning apps. At AfroScholars we want to help change that by providing language learning lessons that are mainly taught using speech in West African Languages.
